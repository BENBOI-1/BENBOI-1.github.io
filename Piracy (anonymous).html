We interviewed a lovely volunteer about pirating. Let's see what Mary, the most piratiest scallywag this side of the internet, has to say!
Interviewer: "Why do you pirate?"
Mary: "Because things are rediculously expensive these days, for example streaming services. It's expected to have multiple, which can add up to an astonishing cost."
Interviewer: "Do you think what you're doing is wrong?"
Mary: "To be honest, it highly depends. If I hire a 10-year-old movie, the money will go to companies I don’t care for. If it were to go to the animators, that would be great.”
Interviewer: "When is it bad to pirate?"
Mary: "Small teams, indie games."
Interviewer: "What do you usually pirate?"
Mary: "Triple A games. They're rediculously expensive. I'm not paying 80 dollars for a game."
Interviewer: "What do you say to those who don't pirate?"
Mary: "That's good, I guess. Money goes towards big coorperations."
Interviewer: "Thank you Mary (We are always polite.)"
Meanwhile, we ALSO interviewed Temujin, a law abiding citizen who does not pirate and definitely did not brutally slaughter 40 million innocent civilians, about his views on pirating.
Interviewer: "Why don't you pirate?"
Temujin: "It’s unethical. People worked hard, they deserve adequate recognition."
Interviewer: "Is pirating wrong?"
Temujin: "Yes. It’s a shame for actors, animators and such stakeholders. When it comes to bigger corporations, it doesn’t matter. "
Interviewers: "When is it bad to pirate?"
Temujin: "If someone’s gaining financial advantage, that’s detrimental to someone else. One person pirating a movie is okay, but if one person distributes it, the person who made it doesn’t get the money."
Interviewers: "What do you say to those who pirate?"
Temujin: "Arr."
Interviewer: "Thank you Genghis-chan Owo."
