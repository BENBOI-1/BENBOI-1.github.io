Digital Piracy refers to the unauthorized duplication of copyrighted content that is sold at lower prices in the ‘grey’ market. Piracy has become more rampant because of the easy access of technology. Piracy also refers to the distribution and use of copyrighted materials, so, even if someone does not take part in the duplication process of piracy, simply distributing it or using it is a crime which may result in legal penalties.

Laws have been created to try to prevent piracy and protect copyrighted works and their privacy. Despite this, the laws on piracy depend on the country: more developed countries have strict laws for piracy, meanwhile countries with more pressing matters do not give piracy the attention it needs. The IT and music industries are the most active industries involved in preventing piracy. With the help of the police, raids on organizations that pirate are conducted. Despite this, penalties are not harsh enough to eliminate the issue at all.
